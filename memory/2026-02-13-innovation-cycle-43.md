## Key Decisions

### 1. Mobile-First Strategy

**Decision:** Build mobile app before desktop v2  
**Rationale:**
- Mobile engagement 50x higher than desktop
- TikTok model proven for virality
- Younger audience (18-35) is mobile-native
- App store distribution = new user source

**Trade-offs:**
- Desktop users may feel neglected (short-term)
- More platform to maintain (iOS + Android)
- App store approval delays

**Mitigation:**
- Progressive web app (PWA) for desktop
- Mobile-responsive web still works
- Unified codebase (React Native Web)

---

### 2. UGC Over AI Perfection

**Decision:** Prioritize user-generated remixes over perfect AI stories  
**Rationale:**
- Infinite content loop (never run out of chapters)
- Network effects (users invest time = retention)
- Viral sharing (creators promote their work)
- Diversifies narrative styles

**Trade-offs:**
- Quality variance (some remixes will be bad)
- Moderation needed (spam, offensive content)
- AI might lose "authority"

**Mitigation:**
- Minimum quality bar (200 words, coherence check)
- Community voting filters bad remixes
- AI still has 60% voting power (maintains quality)

---

### 3. Real-World Integration

**Decision:** Use Chainlink oracles for BTC/ETH prices  
**Rationale:**
- Proven infrastructure (battle-tested)
- Decentralized (no single point of failure)
- Media attention (real-world + fiction hook)

**Trade-offs:**
- Oracle fees (~$100/event)
- Complexity (TEE for custom data)
- Potential manipulation (though Chainlink mitigates)

**Mitigation:**
- Multi-sig verification for custom oracles
- Fallback to manual resolution if oracle fails
- Insurance fund for disputes

---

### 4. Tournament Economics

**Decision:** $50 entry, $10K prize pool, top 100 paid  
**Rationale:**
- Low barrier ($50 = accessible)
- High incentive ($2,500 top prize = motivating)
- Many winners (100 = realistic odds)
- Recurring revenue (monthly)

**Trade-offs:**
- Platform must seed initial prize pool
- Sybil risk (create multiple accounts)
- Complexity (points calculation, anti-cheat)

**Mitigation:**
- Wallet verification (1 entry per verified wallet)
- Staking requirement (burn $FORGE to enter)
- Anomaly detection (flag suspicious patterns)

---

### 5. Showdown Rarity

**Decision:** Allow 2-5 showdowns per month (not every chapter)  
**Rationale:**
- Scarcity = value (each showdown is special)
- Viral when rare (not fatiguing if daily)
- AI maintains narrative authority

**Trade-offs:**
- Lower showdown revenue potential
- Community frustration if challenge fails

**Mitigation:**
- Clear threshold (100 pledgers = guaranteed showdown)
- AI quality bar (bad AI choices â†’ easier to challenge)
- Refunds if challenge fails

## Risks & Mitigations

### Technical Risks

**1. Smart Contract Exploit (CRITICAL)**
- **Risk:** Hacker drains betting pools
- **Impact:** Loss of all funds, reputation destroyed
- **Probability:** Medium (5-10% without audit)
- **Mitigation:**
  - 3 independent audits ($75K)
  - Bug bounty program ($50K reward)
  - Pause functionality (emergency stop)
  - Gradual rollout (max $10K/pool initially)

**2. Scalability (HIGH)**
- **Risk:** Can't handle 50K concurrent users
- **Impact:** Slow load times, failed transactions
- **Probability:** High (likely without prep)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Load testing at 10K users
  - CDN for static assets
  - Redis caching (query results)
  - Database connection pooling
  - Horizontal scaling (Kubernetes)

**3. AI Hallucinations (MEDIUM)**
- **Risk:** AI makes nonsensical narrative choices
- **Impact:** Story quality degrades, users leave
- **Probability:** Medium (GPT-4 hallucinations ~5%)
- **Mitigation:**
  - Multi-model consensus (GPT-4 + Claude)
  - Coherence scoring threshold
  - Human review for critical decisions
  - Community override (showdowns)

## Key Learnings

### 1. Virality > Depth

**Before:** Focus on better AI, more features  
**After:** Focus on viral loops, network effects

**Insight:**
- Perfect AI doesn't matter if no one shares
- Good enough product + viral mechanics > perfect product + no sharing
- TikTok model beats Netflix model for growth

---

### 2. Mobile Wins

**Before:** Desktop-first (big screens for reading)  
**After:** Mobile-first (swipe > scroll)

**Insight:**
- 80% of users are mobile-first (18-35 age group)
- Infinite scroll = addiction (TikTok proved it)
- Mobile sharing = viral distribution (WhatsApp, Twitter)

---

### 3. Real-World Hooks = Media

**Before:** Self-contained fictional narratives  
**After:** Fiction tied to real events

**Insight:**
- "Bitcoin price affects AI story" = headline
- "Readers bet $500K on UN vote outcome" = media coverage
- Mainstream appeal > crypto echo chamber

---

### 4. Competition > Cooperation

**Before:** Individual betting (no social dynamics)  
**After:** Tournaments, leaderboards, status games

**Insight:**
- Humans are competitive (leaderboard = engagement)
- FOMO works ("I could win $10K")
- Status games > intrinsic motivation

---

### 5. Agency > Consumption

**Before:** AI always wins (readers spectate)  
**After:** Readers can override AI (participation)

**Insight:**
- Empowerment = retention (users feel heard)
- Rare wins > frequent wins (scarcity creates value)
- David vs Goliath = viral moments
